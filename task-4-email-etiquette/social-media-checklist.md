# Social Media Checklist for College Students

Navigating social media as a student requires a balance between personal expression and professional reputation. Use this checklist to ensure your digital footprint remains an asset rather than a liability.

## The Checklist

| Do's | Don'ts |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit your privacy settings** regularly to control who sees your personal life. | **Post in the heat of the moment** or when you are feeling overly emotional or angry. |
| **Network professionally** by connecting with professors, alumni, and industry leaders on LinkedIn. | **Share illegal or questionable content**, including underage drinking or illicit substances. |
| **Think before you tag.** Ask friends for permission before posting photos of them. | **Engage with "trolls" or cyberbullies.** Block and report instead of escalating. |
| **Use your platform for good** by sharing campus events, achievements, or causes you care about. | **Bad-mouth your university or employer.** Venting online can lead to disciplinary action. |
| **Check your "Digital Shadow."** Google yourself occasionally to see what recruiters might find. | **Overshare personal details**, such as your specific dorm room number or daily schedule. |

---

> **Pro-Tip:** A good rule of thumb is the "Billboard Test." If you wouldn't be comfortable seeing your post on a giant billboard in the center of campus, don't post it.
