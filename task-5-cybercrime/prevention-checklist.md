# Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for College Students in India

Navigating campus life often means being constantly connected. Whether you are using public Wi-Fi or making quick UPI payments at the canteen, staying cyber-secure is essential. Use this checklist to protect your digital identity and finances.

---

### 🛡️ The Digital Safety Checklist

* **[ ] Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Secure your Gmail, Instagram, and WhatsApp accounts using 2FA. Whenever possible, use authenticator apps (like Google Authenticator) instead of SMS codes to prevent SIM-swapping attacks.
* **[ ] Secure Your UPI Transactions:** Remember that **you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money**. If someone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to "receive a refund" or "claim a prize," it is a scam.
* **[ ] Verify Before You Click (Phishing):** Be wary of emails or WhatsApp messages claiming you’ve won a scholarship or that your bank account is blocked. Check the sender's email address carefully and avoid clicking on suspicious links.
* **[ ] Use Unique Passwords for Every Portal:** Do not use the same password for your college ERP portal and your personal email. Use a trusted Password Manager to keep track of complex, unique passwords.
* **[ ] Hide Your Digital Footprint on Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into banking apps or sensitive student portals while using free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If you must use it, use a reliable VPN to encrypt your data.
* **[ ] Guard Your SIM and Aadhaar Details:** Never share copies of your Aadhaar card or OTPs with unknown individuals promising "free SIM cards" or "quick student loans" on campus. 
* **[ ] Audit Your UPI App Permissions:** Regularly check which third-party apps have access to your UPI ID or bank details. Also, set a **daily transaction limit** on your bank app to minimize potential losses.
* **[ ] Be Skeptical of "High-Paying" Remote Internships:** If an internship offer via Telegram or WhatsApp asks you to pay a "security deposit" or "registration fee" in advance, it is likely a fraudulent scheme.

---

### 🚨 How to Report Cyber Crime

If you or a friend have been cheated online or are facing cyber-harassment, act immediately. Reporting quickly can help in freezing fraudulent transactions.

* **National Helpline:** Call **1930** (Available 24/7)
* **Official Portal:** Report the incident at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
